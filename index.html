The problem we are solving

Your language deserves a keyboard — but making one is hard

You know the sounds and letters of your language better than anyone. But right now, getting a keyboard into people's hands requires a programmer. Keyboard Studio is being built to change that.

The way it works today

  • You need to hire or find a programmer just to get started.
  • Lots of files and settings have to be kept in sync by hand.
  • Your keyboard has to pass a long checklist before it is accepted.
  • One small mistake can mean starting over.

What Keyboard Studio does instead

  • You answer simple questions about your language — no programming.
  • We create all the files automatically and keep them up to date.
  • Built-in checks catch problems before you submit.
  • When you are ready, one click gets your keyboard out the door.

Behind the scenes

We figure out the right setup for your language

Every language is different. Some need simple letter swaps. Others need two-key combinations to produce special characters. Keyboard Studio quietly picks the right approach for you — based on how you describe your language — from twelve tried-and-tested patterns.

• Swap one key for another • Two-key combinations • Type a short sequence • Look up from a list • Spell it out phonetically • Chain multiple key presses • Cycle through accent marks • Hold a key for extra letters • Smart context-aware rules • Restrict certain combinations • Toggle a mode on and off • Connect to a system input method
You say: "I press a quote key, then a vowel, to add an accent mark."
We understand: Two-key combination (press one key, then another to produce a special character)
Studio does: Sets up your keyboard so that quote + a gives you a with the right accent, and so on for every vowel.
You see: A live keyboard to tap and confirm. When it looks right, you move on.

You describe it, we build it

You do not need to know how any of this works under the hood. Keyboard Studio listens to how you describe your language and does the right thing automatically.

If the first suggestion does not feel right, you can try a different approach — and the studio will rebuild your keyboard on the spot.
